Nyama Choma - A Kenyan Delicacy

Nyama Choma is a popular Kenyan dish consisting of grilled meat, usually goat or beef, seasoned simply with salt and slow-roasted over an open flame. It is often enjoyed with ugali, kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad), and a cold drink, making it a favorite for social gatherings.

How to make Nyama Choma

Ingredients

  • Meat (goat, beef, or chicken) 1 kg, cut into chunks or strips
  • Garlic 3-4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger 1-inch piece, grated
  • Onion 1 large, finely chopped
  • Lemon juice 2 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce 2 tablespoons
  • Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
  • Ground coriander 1 teaspoon
  • Ground cumin 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika 1 teaspoon
  • Chili powder (optional) 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Fresh cilantro (optional) chopped, for garnish
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, grated ginger, chopped onion, lemon juice, soy sauce, vegetable oil, ground coriander, ground cumin, paprika, and chili powder. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Mix everything well to create the marinade.
  2. Marinate the Meat: Add the meat pieces to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well-coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for better flavor.
  3. Prepare the Grill: Heat a grill or barbecue to medium-high heat. If using skewers, thread the marinated meat onto them.
  4. Grill the Meat: Place the marinated meat onto the grill. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking and a nice char on all sides.
  5. Serve: Once the meat is fully cooked and charred to your liking, remove it from the grill. Garnish with fresh cilantro (optional).
  6. Enjoy: Serve the nyama choma with kachumbari (tomato and onion salad), ugali or chapati, and sliced lemon wedges. Enjoy your delicious Kenyan grilled meat!

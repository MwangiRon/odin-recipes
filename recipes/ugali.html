Ugali - A Kenyan Delicacy

Ugali is a staple Kenyan dish made from maize flour and water, cooked into a dense, dough-like consistency. It is commonly served with vegetables, meat, or stews and is eaten by hand, using pieces to scoop up accompaniments. Simple yet filling, ugali is a key part of Kenyan cuisine.

How to make Ugali

Ingredients

  • Maize Flour - 2 cups
  • Water - 4 cups
  • Salt
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Step 1: Boil Water Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a pot over medium heat. Add a pinch of salt if desired.
  2. Step 2: Add Maize Flour Gradually Slowly pour maize flour into the boiling water while stirring continuously with a wooden spoon to prevent lumps.
  3. Step 3: Stir and Cook Keep stirring as the mixture thickens. Reduce the heat to medium and continue stirring to ensure a smooth consistency.
  4. Step 4: Knead and Cook Further Press and fold the ugali against the pot to remove lumps. Let it cook for 5-10 minutes until it forms a firm, dough-like texture.
  5. Step 5: Shape and Serve Shape the ugali into a mound using a wooden spoon, then remove it from the pot. Let it rest for a minute before serving.
